Militarization of Antarctica by Superpower

Militarization of Antarctica by Superpower

The vast tundra of Antarctica are soon to be occupied by government superpowers. Russian militarization of the Arctic has already increased dramatically in recent years, and additional militarization by other nations raises the danger of undermining long-standing Arctic cooperation and shared governance. If militarization persists, friction and secrecy may raise the possibility of misunderstanding, jeopardizing collaboration and leading to the disintegration of the Arctic Council's governance, critical environmental accords, and other key norms. How can we sustain the UN’s relationship with the Arctic Council?

Senkaku Islands Territortial Dispute

Senkaku Islands Territortial Dispute

The Senkaku Islands, also known as Diaoyu Island, are located in northeast Taiwan and east of China. Japan has claimed that the island was an occupation of the Japanese empire since 1895, and it will continue to be an island Japan occupies to this day forward. On the other hand, Both the People’s Republic of China and the Republic of China(Taiwan) claims that the island was discovered by Ming Dynasty China in the 14th century, and was forcefully invaded and colonized by the Empire of Japan during the Sino-Japanese wars. 10 years ago, Chinese patrol boats were sighted by the Japanese government, the controversy and dispute is still going on to this day. The committee is hoping that Taiwan, China, and other United Countries will either try to assuage the problem, or escalate it to full scale war.
